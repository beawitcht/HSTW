Privacy Notice

This notice applies to Hastings Stop The War.

We refer to the above as “Hastings Stop The War”, “we” and “us” in this document unless otherwise specified.

This privacy notice explains how we collect and use personal data.

Who collects your data?

Hastings Stop The War is an unincorporated association.

To contact us about privacy, email us at: info@hastingsstopthewar.org

If you write to us to exercise your rights, we may ask for information to confirm your identity.

What personal data do we collect?

If you sign our "I say NO! to war" pledge, your name, email address, and postcode

Any personal data you volunteer in any free text fields in any paper-based or digital surveys, questionnaires or webforms that are captured by Hastings Stop The War.

Aggregated data, and pseudonymous and anonymous data we have created from your personal data but we are unable to use to directly identify you.

Any communications we have with you which may be over email, SMS, telephone, instant messaging services or face to face.

Posts by or relating to you on social media (X, Meta platforms, YouTube etc.).

Publicly available information on websites incl. Companies House, electoral registers, news and broadcast media or historical publications etc.

Special category data (sensitive data)

Some of the information we process is special category data under the UK GDPR, which needs extra protection. Most notably this includes political opinions, which can be inferred from signing a pledge focused on an anti-war campaign and from campaign engagement.

Where we process special category data, we identify:
1. a lawful basis under Article 6 UK GDPR; and
2. a separate condition under Article 9 UK GDPR.

In the case of your political opinions inferred from you signing the Pledge, we rely on your consent (Art. 6) and explicit consent (Art. 9).

Where do we obtain your data from?

From yourself when you sign our "I say NO! to war" pledge (direct data collection).

From yourself via email, telephone or face to face communications we have with you (direct data collection).

From any photographs/video taken by any photographer or videographer on behalf of Hastings Stop The War at a meeting or event (indirect data collection).

From yourself when you complete a survey or other webform presented to you by Hastings Stop The War or a survey/web-capture provider who is under contract to Hastings Stop The War (indirect data collection) (also, please refer to that platform’s privacy notice).

From other Hastings Stop The War supporters who know you or have information about you (indirect data collection).

From yourself when you have interacted with Hastings Stop The War on the doorstep, during phonebanking, at an event, online, or other (direct data collection).

From publicly available websites including Companies House, social media sites, news outlets, and online publications etc. (indirect data collection).

From public bodies such as regulators (e.g. Information Commissioner’s Office, Electoral Commission, HMRC etc) (indirect data collection).

What do we use your personal data for, and what is our lawful basis?

We exist primarily to inform and mobilise the local community around the anti-war movement. And use this data to build a network of anti-war supporters to collectively call for an end to war.

Consent

Political campaigning communications can amount to unsolicited direct marketing (including promoting aims and ideals). If we send direct marketing by email/text to you, we generally need your explicit consent under PECR, and in practice consent will also be the appropriate UK GDPR lawful basis for that messaging.

We rely on consent for the following:

"I say NO! to war" signatories: to collect your pledge details and contact you (via email, mail, phone, text, instant messaging and or door-knocking) about Hastings Stop The War’s campaign activity (including volunteering, events, and ways to support the anti-war movement).
Special category data (eg political opinions): where explicit consent for the processing described in this notice.

You can withdraw consent at any time (see “What are your privacy rights?” below). Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

Legitimate interests

We rely on legitimate interest for the following:

For operational requirements such as:
running and securing our systems,
limiting access to personal data on a need-to-know basis,
maintaining suppression lists, and
aggregated, non-intrusive internal reporting to understand campaign reach and engagement.

To produce anonymised statistical insights about our reach in the anti-war movement, to help plan and target our campaigning.

If you are on our mailing list: to help ensure our communications are relevant and meaningful, we may supplement the information you provide us with internal notes, tags, lists, filters, or classifications—such as whether you’ve attended an event, opened our recent emails, or engaged with particular campaigns. We do this to understand levels of engagement, segment our audience, and improve how we inform supporters about news, events and ways to get involved. This profiling does not involve automated decision-making that has legal or similarly significant effects.

If you are a supporter: to use the personal data you provide for internal research, statistical analysis, and reporting. This helps us understand our reach, improve our activities, and make informed decisions. We only use this information in aggregate or non-intrusive ways and never use it to make decisions about individuals.

If you are an influencer, pundit, trade union official, leading campaigner or high profile individual: to collect your contact information and information on your views, activities and behaviour where it is publicly available or revealed in our own interactions with you or your networks. and to use that information to contact you on matters relevant to our purposes.

If you are an activist who shares our values and aims: to collect your contact information and information on your views, activities and behaviour, usually directly from you but also sometimes where it is publicly available or revealed in our own interactions with you or your networks. and to use that information to contact you on organisational matters.

If you are a councillor, local candidate, MP or parliamentary candidate who we deem are sympathetic to our mission: to collect your contact information and information on your views, activities and behaviour where it is publicly available or revealed in our own interactions with you or your networks, and to use that information to contact you on matters relevant to our purposes including to support your electoral campaigns.

If you are a journalist: to collect your contact information and information on your journalistic activities for the purpose of briefing you.

Legal obligation

We may process and retain limited information where necessary to:

respond to subject access requests or any other exercise of a data subject’s right under the GDPR.

communicate with regulators (incl. the Information Commissioner’s Office and Electoral Commission) and discharge our compliance duties under relevant legislation.

To communicate with the police or other authorities in criminal matters.

To conduct litigation and legal negotiations.

Who has access to your data?

We will never sell your data.

pledge data is stored in Hastings Stop The War systems and accessed by a small internal team using Google Drive, Google Sheets & Proton Mail.

We may share data with:

Authorised Hastings Stop The War members who need the data for campaign activity.

Regulators and law enforcement where required.

We use the following digital platforms: Google Drive, Google Sheets, Protonmail, SimpleLogin, GitHub, Cloudflare, WhatsApp

How your data is kept secure

We use physical, electronic, and managerial measures designed to protect personal data, including access controls, password/MFA practices, and limiting access to authorised personnel.

How long will we keep your personal data?

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this notice.

"I say NO! to war" pledge data: kept to run the Hastings Stop The War mailing list and campaign communications across relevant anti-war campaigns unless you unsubscribe/withdraw consent or request erasure.

Profiling and automated decision-making

We may tag or segment supporter records (eg by council/area and engagement) so communications are relevant, and to plan campaigning activity. This does not involve automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects.

What are your privacy rights?

Under the UK GDPR you have rights including:

right to be informed
right of access
right to rectification
right to erasure
right to restrict processing
right to object (including an unconditional right to object to direct marketing)
rights relating to automated decision-making and profiling

To exercise your rights, contact info@hastingsstopthewar.org

If you are not satisfied with how we respond, you have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).
